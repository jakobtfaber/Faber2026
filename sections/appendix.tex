\section{Association cards}
\label{app:assoc-cards}

This appendix collects the per-burst association-card figures referenced in the
main text. Each card is a compact, at-a-glance cross-check that the CHIME/FRB and
DSA-110 triggers correspond to the same astrophysical burst, and that the
association used throughout the manuscript is internally consistent.

Across the sample, the cards summarize (i) the timing consistency between the
two pipelines after accounting for barycentric corrections and dispersive delays, (ii)
sky-position consistency between the candidate localizations (to the extent
available for each burst), and (iii) dispersion-measure consistency and
bookkeeping (e.g., the CHIME/FRB-reported DM compared to any independently
reconstructed DM used in downstream analysis). Many cards also include catalog
context (nearby galaxies, foreground structures, or survey footprints) that is
used as a qualitative sanity check on the association and as a pointer to the
foreground-census layer described in the main text.

When reading the cards, the most important failure modes they are designed to
catch are: a mis-identified counterpart between instruments, a bookkeeping
mismatch (wrong TOA, wrong DM, or wrong metadata version), or an association
that is only explainable by implausible timing/DM offsets. We treat the cards as
validation artifacts: they motivate which bursts advance to the joint
DM/scattering analysis, but we avoid over-interpreting any individual panel
beyond confirming that the association inputs are coherent.

\section{Intracluster dispersion measures for foreground clusters}
\label{app:clusters}

This appendix collects the per-cluster hot-gas dispersion-measure columns behind the
$b\le R_{500}$ budget cut described in the main text. Only the single cluster
that pierces the halo enters $\mathrm{DM_{int}}$; the remaining panels show the
excluded clusters and the near-miss that motivate the cut being a principled
impact-parameter threshold rather than a hand-picked selection.
The adopted cluster impact parameter ($b=603.6$\,kpc, $b/R_{500}=0.83$)
descends from the original cluster analysis; recomputing it uniformly from
the registry centroid, the burst position, and the fiducial cosmology---the
convention applied to the galaxy halos---gives $619$\,kpc
($b/R_{500}=0.85$). We carry this $\approx3\%$ provenance difference as a
systematic on the cluster geometry; it corresponds to a $\mp3\%$ shift in
the modeled intracluster column, negligible against the factor-of-two
profile and cluster-parameter systematics quoted in the main text.

The discovery-completeness audit also recovers WHL
J115048.0$+$714428, a WHL12 cluster at $z=0.1893$ whose centroid lies
$\simeq152''$ from the adopted Wen--Han 2024 system and at a comparable
impact parameter. Uniformly recomputing its geometry gives $b=614$\,kpc
(the earlier discovery product gives $614.0$\,kpc; a separate reconstruction
rounded this to $608$\,kpc). The system may be a lower-richness cluster,
substructure, or an alternate centroiding of the same complex; it is not
present in the adopted \citet{WenHan2024} source. We therefore do not pass its
brightest-cluster-galaxy stellar mass through the isolated-galaxy
stellar-to-halo-mass relation, and we assign no second intracluster column:
the required $M_{500}$ and $R_{500}$ are unavailable in the adopted model
input. Its possible contribution is a one-sided, unmodeled systematic.
Including it in $\mathrm{DM_{int}}$ would require a separately validated
WHL12 richness-to-$M_{500}$ calibration and an adjudication of whether the two
catalog entries represent distinct gas halos.

\section{Forward-modeled host dispersion measure}
\label{app:host-forward-model}

The host term of Table~\ref{tab:budget} is a forward-modeled posterior rather
than an arithmetic residual (Section~\ref{sec:dm}). For each redshift-constrained
sightline we draw $N=2\times10^{5}$ Monte-Carlo samples of
$\mathrm{DM_{host}}=\mathrm{DM_{obs}}-\mathrm{DM_{MW,ISM}}-\mathrm{DM_{MW,halo}}
-\mathrm{DM_{cosmic}}-\mathrm{DM_{int}}$, propagating priors on the non-host
terms:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Cosmological.} The diffuse cosmic term is the intergalactic column
$\mathrm{DM_{IGM}}\sim\mathrm{LogNormal}(\mu(z),\sigma(z))$, with $\mu(z)$ and
$\sigma(z)$ the redshift-dependent parameters of the bivariate (IGM, halo)
log-normal fit to a mock FRB survey in IllustrisTNG-300 \citep{Walker2024},
adopted through the \citet{Connor2024} reproduction package. We take the IGM
marginal rather than the total cosmic column
$\mathrm{DM_{cos}}=\mathrm{DM_{IGM}}+\mathrm{DM_X}$, because the identified
intervening halos ($\mathrm{DM_X}$) are already carried by the separate
$\mathrm{DM_{int}}$ census and the total would double-count them
(Section~\ref{sec:dm}). The calibration is rescaled to the IGM baryon fraction
$f_{\rm IGM}=0.76^{+0.10}_{-0.11}$ that \citet{Connor2024} infer from the
DSA-110 and literature FRB sample; we marginalize $f_{\rm IGM}$ over that
asymmetric-normal prior, which shifts the log-median by
$\ln(f_{\rm IGM}/f_{\rm IGM,TNG})$ with $f_{\rm IGM,TNG}=0.797$. This supersedes
the \citet{Macquart2020} $p(\Delta)$ form with $\sigma_{\rm DM}=F\,z^{-1/2}$:
\citet{Connor2024} show that the diffuse scatter is set by non-Poissonian
intersection of IGM filaments and sheets rather than by halo Poisson statistics,
and does not follow the $z^{-1/2}$ scaling \citep{McQuinn2014, James2022}. The
TNG-300 calibration is tabulated for $z\ge0.1$; for the two sightlines below
that redshift (FRB~20220207C, FRB~20240203A) we continue $\mu(z)$ along the
\citet{Macquart2020} redshift dependence,
$\langle\mathrm{DM_{IGM}}\rangle\propto\int_0^z(1+z')/E(z')\,\mathrm{d}z'$,
rather than extrapolating the spline, and hold $\sigma$ at its grid-edge value.
The continuation is exact in the $z\rightarrow0$ limit and reproduces the
tabulated log-median increments to $1.5\%$ over $0.1<z<0.3$.
\item \emph{Galactic disk.} The NE2025 disk column with a $30\%$ lognormal
uncertainty.
\item \emph{Galactic halo.} $40\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ median, lognormal with a
factor-$\sim\!2$ span ($2\sigma$ range $\approx[20,80]\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$;
\citealt{YamasakiTotani2020, KeatingPen2020, Cook2023}).
\item \emph{Intervening.} The Table~\ref{tab:budget} $\mathrm{DM_{int}}$
column with a $40\%$
(measured-mass halo), factor-$2$ (assumed-mass halo), or $30\%$ (cluster)
lognormal uncertainty; after the census remediation every contributing halo
carries a vetted empirical mass, so the galaxy columns use the $40\%$ family
and the assumed-mass factor-$2$ family is currently unexercised.
\end{itemize}
The resulting posteriors are asymmetric, dominated at higher redshift by the
diffuse cosmic scatter (Figure~\ref{fig:dm_host_posteriors},
Table~\ref{tab:host-forward-model}); their medians exceed the
mean-subtracted residuals because the log-normal $P(\mathrm{DM_{IGM}})$ is
right-skewed. One sightline, FRB~20220310F, carries a median
posterior marginally below zero with $P(\mathrm{DM_{host}}<0)\simeq0.5$; it
is consistent with a vanishing host contribution, and the $f_{\rm IGM}$
required to place its median exactly at zero lies within
$0.2\sigma$ of the adopted prior median. The highest-redshift sightline,
FRB~20221203A, is likewise consistent with zero
($P(\mathrm{DM_{host}}<0)=0.24$). The prior widths above are fiducial
and are documented so they can be revised; the analysis and both artifacts are
reproduced by \texttt{scripts/dm\_budget\_uncertainty.py}.
