\section{Association cards}
\label{app:assoc-cards}

This appendix collects the per-burst association-card figures referenced in the
main text. Each card is a compact, at-a-glance cross-check that the CHIME/FRB and
DSA-110 triggers correspond to the same astrophysical burst, and that the
association used throughout the manuscript is internally consistent.

Across the sample, the cards summarize (i) the timing consistency between the
two pipelines after accounting for barycentric corrections and dispersion, (ii)
sky-position consistency between the candidate localizations (to the extent
available for each burst), and (iii) dispersion-measure consistency and
bookkeeping (e.g., the CHIME/FRB-reported DM compared to any independently
reconstructed DM used in downstream analysis). Many cards also include catalog
context (nearby galaxies, foreground structures, or survey footprints) that is
used as a qualitative sanity check on the association and as a pointer to the
foreground-census layer described in the main text.

When reading the cards, the most important failure modes they are designed to
catch are: a mis-identified counterpart between instruments, a bookkeeping
mismatch (wrong TOA, wrong DM, or wrong metadata version), or an association
that is only explainable by implausible timing/DM offsets. We treat the cards as
validation artifacts: they motivate which bursts advance to the joint
DM/scattering analysis, but we avoid over-interpreting any individual panel
beyond confirming that the association inputs are coherent.

\section{Intracluster dispersion for foreground clusters}
\label{app:clusters}

This appendix collects the per-cluster hot-gas dispersion columns behind the
$b\le R_{500}$ budget cut described in the main text. Only the single cluster
that pierces the halo enters $\mathrm{DM_{int}}$; the remaining panels show the
excluded clusters and the near-miss that motivate the cut being a principled
impact-parameter threshold rather than a hand-picked selection.

\begin{figure*}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/clusters_icm.pdf}
    \caption{Predicted intracluster hot-gas dispersion for the four foreground
    clusters along the FRB~20230307A sightline. Each panel plots the
    modified-NFW hot-baryon dispersion column $\mathrm{DM}(b)$ against projected
    impact parameter $b$ (blue); the vertical marker is the sightline's measured
    impact parameter, the dashed line is $R_{500}$, and the shaded region is
    inside $R_{500}$. Only J115120.4$+$714435 ($b/R_{500}=0.83$) meets the
    $b\le R_{500}$ budget cut and enters $\mathrm{DM_{int}}$
    ($\sim$160\,pc\,cm$^{-3}$; Table~\ref{tab:foreground}); the others lie beyond
    $R_{500}$ and are excluded even where the profile remains formally nonzero
    (J115128.2$+$713637 at $b/R_{500}=1.25$, $\sim$95\,pc\,cm$^{-3}$). Cluster
    masses ($\log M_{500}$) and radii are from the \citet{WenHan2024} DESI
    Legacy/WISE catalog.}
    \label{fig:clusters_icm}
\end{figure*}
