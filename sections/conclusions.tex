\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

This circulation draft draws a conclusion only for the foreground census, whose
search and verdicts have passed re-validation. The association and sample
definition are likewise re-validated, but their results text is not yet written;
the DM-budget decomposition, the scattering fits, the scintillation products, the
turbulence parameters, and the burst energies remain withheld. Conclusions on
those follow once their Results slots are repopulated from trusted products.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textit{Foreground census.} Only one of the twelve co-detection sightlines,
that of FRB~20230307A, passes within $R_{500}$ of a foreground cluster. The
intervening circumgalactic and intracluster contribution to the dispersion
budget is therefore appreciable on that sightline alone and small on the rest;
the confirmed foreground galaxies lie in the outer circumgalactic medium, where
individual columns are minor. The census reaches only as deep as the wide-field
catalogs available at this declination, so a sightline without a confirmed
intervening system is unconstrained by survey depth, not demonstrably free of
foreground gas.
\end{itemize}
