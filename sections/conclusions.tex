\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

We have assembled the twelve CHIME/FRB--DSA-110 co-detected FRBs and used their
$\sim$1\,GHz detection baseline to separate, sightline by sightline, the media
that contribute to the observed dispersion and scattering.

\begin{enumerate}
\item The observed DM decomposes for most sightlines into a modest Milky-Way term
($\mathrm{DM_{MW}}=78$--$171\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$) and a cosmological mean that
grows with redshift to dominate the higher-$z$ bursts; confirmed foreground
systems add a modeled circumgalactic column on eight sightlines, only one of
which (FRB~20230814B) pierces a galaxy interior
(Section~\ref{sec:results-budget}).
\item The host residual is formally negative only for the two highest-redshift
sightlines (FRB~20220310F, FRB~20221203A), whose budgets are dominated by the
cosmological mean, so the negative residual bounds the sightline-to-sightline
scatter about the Macquart relation rather than the circumgalactic model
(Section~\ref{sec:foreground}).
\item A foreground search of the twelve fields returns 49 candidate systems
(29 confirmed foreground), establishing which sightlines can carry an intervening
dispersion or scattering term (Table~\ref{tab:foreground}); two sightlines lie
outside the deep-imaging footprints, where the intervening term is
unconstrained by the searched surveys rather than excluded.
\item The uniform $\beta$-based joint re-fit is complete across the sample:
two sightlines return interior turbulence spectra
(FRB~20230325A, $\beta=3.72$, just above Kolmogorov; FRB~20230307A,
$\beta=3.23$, the steepest frequency scaling in the sample), nine rail at the
square-law limit and are quoted as geometry-conditioned $\alpha=4$ limits, and
one fails the joint-fit quality gate (Section~\ref{sec:results-alpha}).
\item The measured $\tau_{1\,\mathrm{GHz}}$ exceeds the predicted intervening
pulse-broadening by $1.5$--$3$ orders of magnitude on ten of the eleven
measured sightlines, pointing to host-dominated scattering; the exception,
FRB~20230913A, is nominated as intervening-dominated by two independent
diagnostics---its foreground halo predicts $\approx0.7$ of the measured
$\tau$, and it is the only sightline whose scintillation--scattering product
is consistent with a single screen (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}).
\item A diffractive-scintillation excess of $\sim$7--11$\times$ the NE2025
Galactic floor on the mid-latitude sightlines, robust across
scattering-calibrated electron-density models, points to an extragalactic (host
or intervening) scattering screen rather than a Milky-Way origin; sample-wide,
the single-screen product $\tau\,\Delta\nu_d$ shows the resolved scintillation
and the pulse broadening arise on different screens for every sightline but
FRB~20230913A (Section~\ref{sec:results-scintillation}).
\item Modeling hidden temporal sub-components is essential to an unbiased
turbulence spectrum: an unmodeled companion is absorbed into the scattering tail
and biases $\beta$ away from its true value, distorting both the fitted PBF
shape and the frequency scaling simultaneously (Section~\ref{sec:disc-alpha}).
\item For eight sightlines with a spectroscopic host redshift and a
quality-passing joint fit we measure band-restricted isotropic-equivalent
energies of $5\times10^{38}$--$1\times10^{41}\,\mathrm{erg}$
(Section~\ref{sec:burst-energies}).
\end{enumerate}

The principal open item is physical rather than observational: the nine
$\beta=4$-railed sightlines are consistent with the square-law
(exponential-PBF) limit of the thin-screen family, and deciding whether an
extended-medium pulse-broadening function fits them without railing---turning
the geometry-conditioned $\alpha=4$ limits into measurements---is the natural
next analysis. Population-level $\beta$ statistics beyond the census counts
reported here (a median, or fractions by class) are deliberately withheld
while that geometry question is open.
