\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

We draw conclusions here for the association tests, the foreground census, and
the dispersion-measure budget decomposition; conclusions on the scattering fits, the
scintillation products, the turbulence parameters, and the burst energies follow
in the corresponding sections.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textit{Association.} All twelve candidate pairs are genuine
co-detections: each has a chance-coincidence probability $P_{\rm cc}<10^{-8}$
under conservative, chance-maximizing windows and timing residuals consistent with
the inter-site geometric delay under the shared DSA-DM reference convention.
Eight of the twelve additionally pass an independent CHIME--DSA DM-agreement
check; the other four rest on position and timing alone.

\item \textit{Foreground census.} The FRB~20230307A sightline is the only one in
the twelve-burst sample that passes within $R_{500}$ of a foreground cluster,
contributing an intracluster column of order $160\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$
(uncertain at the factor-of-two level, $\approx100$--$560\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$
across the profile choice and the cluster-parameter systematics).
The intervening circumgalactic and intracluster contribution to the
dispersion-measure budget is therefore appreciable on that sightline alone and
small on the rest;
the confirmed foreground galaxies lie in the outer circumgalactic medium, where
individual columns are minor. The census reaches only as deep as the wide-field
catalogs available at this declination, so a sightline without a confirmed
intervening system is unconstrained by survey depth, not demonstrably free of
foreground gas.

\item \textit{Dispersion-measure budget.} With $\mathrm{DM_{host}}$ forward-modeled as a
posterior rather than an arithmetic residual, the decomposition leaves host
contributions that are a minority of the extragalactic column on every
redshift-constrained sightline (median $\mathrm{DM_{host}}$ from
$\approx-10$ to $200\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$, with broad cosmology-dominated
uncertainties): the extragalactic column is dominated by the cosmological
term, not the host. The intervening term is concentrated on the single
cluster-crossing sightline, and the two highest-redshift sightlines are
consistent with zero host ($P(\mathrm{DM_{host}}<0)\approx0.5$), their
marginally negative medians reflecting scatter about the diffuse cosmic
normalization rather than an unphysical host. Sightlines without spectroscopic
host redshifts are excluded from all distance-dependent statements.
\end{itemize}
