\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

We have assembled the twelve CHIME/FRB--DSA-110 co-detected FRBs and used their
$\sim$1\,GHz detection baseline to separate, sightline by sightline, the media
that contribute to the observed dispersion and scattering.

\begin{enumerate}
\item The observed DM decomposes for most sightlines into a modest Milky-Way term
($\mathrm{DM_{MW}}=78$--$171\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$) and a cosmological mean that
grows with redshift to dominate the higher-$z$ bursts, with no intervening
contribution required (Section~\ref{sec:results-budget}).
\item An intervening circumgalactic column is significant only for the three
sightlines that pass through a foreground galaxy's interior
($b<0.1\,R_{\mathrm{vir}}$); on four galaxy-interior sightlines the host residual
is formally negative, which bounds the most uncertain (capped) circumgalactic
model rather than the host (Section~\ref{sec:foreground}).
\item A foreground search of the twelve fields returns 49 candidate systems
(29 confirmed foreground), establishing which sightlines can carry an intervening
dispersion or scattering term (Table~\ref{tab:foreground}).
\item A diffractive-scintillation excess of $\sim$7--11$\times$ the NE2025
Galactic floor on the mid-latitude sightlines, robust across
scattering-calibrated electron-density models, points to an extragalactic (host
or intervening) scattering screen rather than a Milky-Way origin
(Section~\ref{sec:scattering}).
\item Modeling hidden temporal sub-components is essential to an unbiased
scattering index: an unmodeled companion is absorbed into the scattering tail and
biases $\alpha$ away from its true value (Section~\ref{sec:disc-alpha}).
\item For the eight sightlines with a spectroscopic host redshift we measure
band-restricted isotropic-equivalent energies of
$5\times10^{38}$--$1\times10^{41}\,\mathrm{erg}$ (Table~\ref{tab:burst-energies}).
\end{enumerate}

The principal step still outstanding is to finalize the per-sightline scattering
time and complete the measured-versus-predicted overlay of
Figure~\ref{fig:budget}: the uniform multi-component re-fit already constrains the
clean single-screen sightlines, and locking the marginal ones will turn the
predicted intervening pulse-broadening into a direct, per-sightline test of where
each burst is scattered.
