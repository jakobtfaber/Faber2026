\subsection{Burst multiplicity as a systematic on the scattering index}
\label{sec:disc-alpha}

The result of Section~\ref{sec:results-alpha} is a cautionary systematic for any
single-component scattering index. A temporal sub-component omitted from the
model is partially absorbed into the scattering tail, biasing $\alpha$ high: in
the case we examined the bias is $\Delta\alpha\approx0.56$, many times the formal
uncertainty. Because $\alpha$ is the quantity that distinguishes a Kolmogorov
turbulent screen ($\alpha\simeq4.4$; \citealp{Rickett1990}) from a shallower
spectrum, an unrecognized companion can masquerade as anomalous,
sub-Kolmogorov scattering---a regime in which measured pulse-broadening indices
already scatter well below the Kolmogorov value
\citep{Bhat2004}. We verified
that the favored second component is not an artifact of the likelihood:
reconstructing the single-component residual reproduces the whiteness statistic
that flagged the burst, the evidence gain is free of the eigenvalue-conditioning
pathology that a spurious near-degenerate component would trigger, and the
companion sits well inside the resolvable separation. Even the two-component
model, however, does not fully whiten the residual---a sharp rising edge
survives, consistent with an intrinsic fast rise, a limitation of the
scattered-pulse kernel, or a third component---so the corrected $\alpha$ should
be read as better-conditioned rather than final. A systematic re-fit of the
flagged co-detections in our sample is underway.
