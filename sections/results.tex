\subsubsection{Dominant intervening systems}
\label{sec:dominant-systems}

Of the twelve sightlines, only one crosses a foreground cluster within its
characteristic radius. The FRB~20230307A sightline passes at $b/R_{500}=0.83$
from the \citet{WenHan2024} cluster J115120.4$+$714435 ($z=0.200$,
$\log M_{500}=14.1$), for which the two-phase modified-NFW model predicts an
intracluster column of order $160\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$
(Figure~\ref{fig:clusters_icm}). This is the single dominant intervening system
in the sample. The other foreground clusters recovered along this and the two
other cluster-bearing sightlines all lie beyond $R_{500}$, where the modeled
column drops steeply; they are excluded from the budget even where the profile
remains formally nonzero, and none approaches the FRB~20230307A cluster in
predicted contribution.

The confirmed foreground galaxies lie at impact parameters of tens to a few
hundred kpc, in the outer circumgalactic medium rather than the galaxy interior.
Each therefore adds only a small dispersion, and no single galaxy halo dominates
its sightline's intervening column.
The intervening dispersion in this sample is thus concentrated almost entirely on
the one cluster-crossing sightline and is small on the rest; the sightlines
carrying no confirmed within-aperture system are limited by survey depth
(Section~\ref{sec:obs-fg}), not shown to be free of foreground gas.

The component-multiplicity audit is already informative as a validation step,
even before the turbulence parameters are restored as citable measurements.
Figure~\ref{fig:jointmodel-pair-whitney} shows the current FRB~20220310F
(\textit{whitney}) two-dimensional data/model/residual panel after the CHIME
side is forced to retain its resolved second pulse. The full set of eleven
current joint-model morphology-audit panels is included in
Appendix~\ref{app:jointmodel-pairs}. We use these figures to check whether the
adopted component count leaves coherent burst power in the residuals; we do not
yet quote the fitted scattering or turbulence parameters from them.
