\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The association tests, the foreground census, and the dispersion-measure budget
decomposition are reported below. The scattering fits for individual sightlines,
the scintillation products, the turbulence parameters, the burst energies, and
the measured-versus-predicted scattering comparison are presented in the
following subsections.

\subsection{Association of the co-detections}
\label{sec:results-association}

All twelve candidate pairs pass the association tests of
Section~\ref{sec:toa}. Under the conservative, chance-maximizing windows of
Section~\ref{sec:toa-pcc}, every pair has $P_{\rm cc}<10^{-6}$ and passes the
positional-coincidence check; the
timing residuals, referenced to
400\,MHz under the shared DSA-DM convention, are consistent with the
predicted inter-site geometric delay (Table~\ref{tab:sample};
Figure~\ref{fig:association-summary}). The original association calculation
used a DM-conditioned chance term for eight events and deliberately retained
$f_{\rm DM}=1$ for the other four. The new phase-coherence analysis now yields
independent CHIME and DSA measurements for all twelve, with
$|\mathrm{DM_{CHIME}}-\mathrm{DM_{DSA}}|\leq
0.117\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ (Table~\ref{tab:dm-measurements}). We do not use
this post-selection result to reduce the published chance probabilities for
the four conservative position-and-time associations. We therefore treat all
twelve pairs as genuine co-detections in what follows.

\subsection{Per-sightline dispersion-measure budget}
\label{sec:results-budget}
\label{sec:budget}

Table~\ref{tab:budget} reports the decomposition of Eq.~\ref{eq:dmbudget}
along the twelve sightlines. Three features organize the sample. First, the
intervening term is strongly concentrated: summed over the confirmed
foreground systems of Section~\ref{sec:obs-fg}, $\mathrm{DM_{int}}$ exceeds
$100\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ on only one sightline (FRB~20230307A,
$241\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$, dominated by the intracluster column of
Section~\ref{sec:dominant-systems}), while the remaining sightlines carry
$\lesssim 85\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ and four tabulate
$\mathrm{DM_{int}}=0$---for two distinct reasons. FRB~20220506D and
FRB~20240122A lie outside the deep-imaging footprints, so their sightlines
are constrained only by the shallow all-sky layers and their intervening
terms are unconstrained rather than measured to be zero (note~u). FRB~20240203A
and FRB~20240229A carry confirmed foreground halos, but those halos lie
beyond the virial radii implied by their assumed masses, so the modeled
columns vanish; their zeros are conditional on the mass assumption (note~m),
and FRB~20240203A is additionally outside the deep footprints. One sightline
(FRB~20230814B) crosses a foreground halo in the
galaxy-interior regime ($b \approx 4.6$\,kpc), where the reported column is
capped at its $b=0.1\,R_{\rm vir}$ value rather than extrapolated through the
core. Second, the forward-modeled host posteriors
(Section~\ref{sec:dm}, Appendix~\ref{app:host-forward-model}) are broad and
asymmetric, dominated by the cosmological scatter: their medians span
$\mathrm{DM_{host}}\approx-10$--$200\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$, the largest on
FRB~20240229A ($198^{+59}_{-76}\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$), and every
redshift-known sightline is consistent with a host contributing a minority of
the extragalactic column once the width of the cosmological term is folded in.
Third, the two
highest-redshift sightlines (FRB~20220310F, FRB~20221203A), whose
mean-subtracted point residuals were negative, have host posteriors consistent
with zero ($P(\mathrm{DM_{host}}<0)\approx0.5$; Table~\ref{tab:budget}); their
marginally negative medians reflect sightline-to-sightline scatter about the
diffuse cosmic normalization at these redshifts, not an unphysical host, and an
IGM baryon fraction within $0.25\sigma$ of the adopted prior places either
median at zero. The three sightlines without
spectroscopic host redshifts are excluded from all distance-dependent terms
(Table~\ref{tab:budget}, note p).

The predicted intervening pulse broadening from the thin-screen two-phase
model is small everywhere: $\tau_{\mathrm{int}} \le 0.024$\,ms at 1\,GHz,
with the largest values on the sightlines whose confirmed halos are crossed
at the smallest impact parameters. The comparison of these predictions with
the measured scattering times is deferred to the scattering analysis
(Section~\ref{sec:results-alpha}).

\subsubsection{Dominant intervening systems}
\label{sec:dominant-systems}

Of the twelve sightlines, only one crosses a foreground cluster within its
characteristic radius $R_{500}$, the radius within which the mean enclosed
density is 500 times the critical density at the cluster redshift
(Section~\ref{sec:obs-fg}). The FRB~20230307A sightline passes at $b/R_{500}=0.83$
from the \citet{WenHan2024} cluster J115120.4$+$714435 ($z=0.200$,
$\log M_{500}=14.1$), for which the two-phase modified-NFW model predicts an
intracluster column of order $160\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$
(Figure~\ref{fig:clusters_icm}). This column is uncertain at the factor-of-two
level: an X-ray/SZ-motivated isothermal $\beta$-model with the same $M_{500}$
and a cosmic gas fraction gives a somewhat higher central value
($\approx250\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$), and propagating the richness-based
$M_{500}$ ($\sim\!0.2$\,dex scatter), the gas fraction ($f_{\rm gas}=0.10$--$0.16$),
and the profile shape spans $\approx100$--$560\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$
(Appendix~\ref{app:host-forward-model}). The mNFW profile is calibrated on
$\sim\!L_\star$ halos \citep{ProchaskaZheng2019} and is not guaranteed to hold at
$\log M_{500}=14.1$; its value sits near the low end of the $\beta$-model range
and should be read as a conservative estimate. This is nonetheless the single
dominant intervening system in the sample. The other foreground clusters recovered along this and the two
other cluster-bearing sightlines all lie beyond $R_{500}$, where the modeled
column drops steeply; they are excluded from the budget even where the profile
remains formally nonzero, and none approaches the FRB~20230307A cluster in
predicted contribution.

The confirmed foreground galaxies lie at impact parameters of tens to a few
hundred kpc, in the outer circumgalactic medium rather than the galaxy interior.
Each therefore adds only a small dispersion-measure contribution, and no single galaxy halo dominates
its sightline's intervening column.
The intervening DM in this sample is thus concentrated almost entirely on
the one cluster-crossing sightline and is small on the rest; the sightlines
carrying no confirmed within-aperture system are limited by survey depth
(Section~\ref{sec:obs-fg}), not shown to be free of foreground gas.

\subsection{Scintillation and screen attribution}
\label{sec:results-scintillation}

We report here the revalidated DSA-band diffractive-scintillation products
together with the outcome of the CHIME-band
scintillation campaign; the scattering fits that enter any
$\tau\,\Delta\nu_d$ screen product and the resulting one- versus two-screen
attribution are deferred to Section~\ref{sec:disc-screen-attribution} once
their prerequisite strands are independently trusted. The estimator used for the decorrelation bandwidth and
modulation index, and the two-screen framework that the attribution will draw
on---including the $\tau_{s}\leftrightarrow\nu_{s}$ relation, the two-screen
ACF product term, and the screen-distance constraint---are set out in
Appendix~\ref{app:twoscreen}.

For each burst we recompute the spectral autocorrelation function (ACF) of the
DSA-110 dynamic spectrum in equal-signal-to-noise frequency sub-bands and fit
it with a sum of Lorentzian components, whose half-width at half-maximum defines
the diffractive decorrelation bandwidth $\gamma$
\citep{Rickett1990, Cordes2016}. The number of sub-bands and the number of
Lorentzian components per sub-band are selected internally, from fixed
model-comparison and viability gates rather than inherited fit choices, as
described in Section~\ref{sec:methods}; components that fail the bandwidth,
modulation, or fractional-uncertainty quality flags are retained in the
component tables but excluded from the summary measurements.

Across the twelve co-detections, the regenerated fit diagnostics contain at
least one unflagged DSA-band decorrelation-bandwidth measurement per burst, for
a total of 41 clean Lorentzian
components across the sample. Individual clean components span
$0.129$--$23.4$\,MHz across the $1.28$--$1.53$\,GHz DSA band. The upper-left
panel of each per-burst diagnostic (Appendix~\ref{app:dsa-scint-acf}) fits a
power law only to the narrow $\gamma_1$ component track; broader selected
components are shown separately below and excluded from that scaling fit. For
the nine bursts with at least two narrow-component frequencies, the fitted
within-band slope is retained as a descriptive interpolation, not a physical
turbulence-index measurement: the DSA-only frequency lever arm is too short,
and no CHIME-band decorrelation-bandwidth anchor survives the artifact controls
below. Each diagnostic also shows the bandwidth predicted from the accepted
DM-locked pulse-broadening fit through
$\Delta\nu_d=C_1/(2\pi\tau)$ with $C_1=1.16$; the comparison is used only after
the pulse-broadening fit clears its independent residual gates.

The clean-component label is a fit-quality classification, not by itself a
certified astrophysical measurement. A targeted frozen qualification of the
strongest candidate clears the full calibration battery. For the low-frequency
DSA-110 sub-band of FRB~20220506D, centered at $1328.24\,$MHz, we measure
\begin{equation}
    \gamma = 0.446\ \mathrm{MHz}, \qquad
    68\%\ \mathrm{CI} = [0.196,\,0.685]\ \mathrm{MHz}.
\end{equation}
The direct bounded fit is $0.40065\,$MHz; the reported value and interval are
obtained by inverting a deterministic real-background injection response over
13 truth widths (1024 trials). The measurement passes an independent
off-pulse matched-template null, fit-window and low-lag stability checks,
monotonic response calibration, and a resolved-interval requirement. It is the
only presently certified DSA-band scintillation measurement in the sample and,
as a single sub-band point, does not determine a frequency-scaling index.

\paragraph{CHIME-band campaign.} We attempted the same decorrelation-bandwidth
measurement on the up-channelized CHIME/FRB baseband products of all twelve
co-detections, using the estimator lineage recovered from the original
collaboration analysis (frequency-ACF Lorentzian fits in
equal-signal-to-noise sub-bands, half-width-at-half-maximum convention, with
the zero-lag and first-lag bins excluded from the fit). Because the CHIME
up-channelization imprints frequency structure that can mimic narrow
scintillation---a coarse-channel harmonic comb, bandpass scalloping, and
low-lag correlations documented previously for FRB~20230325A---every fit was
subjected to a set of fail-closed artifact controls: an off-pulse null test
(the same fit applied to off-burst spectra must not reproduce the on-burst
width), a two-sided low-lag excision test (the fitted width must be stable,
neither collapsing nor growing by more than a factor of two, when the lowest
lags are removed), a modulation-index physicality bound ($m\lesssim1$ for
diffractive scintillation of a point source, enforced with a tolerance of
$m\leq1.5$ for noisy fits), a minimum of three valid
sub-bands, and a provenance requirement that the input product carry the full
instrumental mitigation stack. A verdict that cannot be evaluated is treated
as a failure, never as a pass.

No CHIME-band decorrelation bandwidth survives these controls: all twelve
bursts are demoted to diagnostic status, each with a named physical cause
(Table~\ref{tab:chime_scint_gates}). For FRB~20230325A we first ran a
factor-isolation sweep over the preprocessing mode and the first fitted lag
(six variants); the fitted widths remain pinned at $36$--$45\,$kHz, clustered
just above the previously documented $35\,$kHz instrumental scale. We then
formed four independent-time ACF streams from the two polarizations and
subtracted a leave-one-window-out off-pulse instrumental template. This
correction passes its independent noise null, demonstrating that the repeatable
instrumental covariance can be removed. Real-background injections nevertheless
recover all tested widths only at modulation index $m=1$ and fail at
$m=0.15$, $0.20$, and $0.30$; the burst diagnostic gives only
$m\simeq0.15$--$0.17$, and its fitted width moves with the fit boundary with an
uncertainty larger than the value. The corrected product therefore remains
below the estimator's demonstrated sensitivity rather than yielding a sky
measurement. The other eleven bursts still lack an equivalently validated
instrumental-background product. The CHIME-band data consequently contribute
no certified $\Delta\nu_d$ and no scaling-index anchor to the screen analysis.

The component-multiplicity audit exposes limitations of the joint-fit
morphology independent of the turbulence-parameter values themselves.
Figure~\ref{fig:codetection-triptych-zach} provides the representative
main-text audit, and Appendix~\ref{app:jointmodel-pairs} shows the ten other
DM-locked model audits. Seven fits pass both the posterior-predictive and visual
residual checks. Four displayed models---FRBs~20220207C, 20221203A, 20230913A,
and 20240229A---remain morphology-only or rejected because coherent residual
structure survives the best tested component count; FRB~20240203A has no
retained joint-model panel. We therefore show all usable audit products but
promote scattering predictions only from the seven visually accepted fits.
