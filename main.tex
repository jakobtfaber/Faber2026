\begin{abstract}
We present a joint dispersion- and scattering-budget analysis of the twelve fast
radio bursts (FRBs) co-detected by CHIME/FRB ($0.4$--$0.8$\,GHz) and DSA-110
($\sim$1.4\,GHz) between 2022 February and 2024 February, spanning
$\mathrm{DM_{obs}}=262$--$960\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$. The $\sim$1\,GHz baseline
between the two detections breaks the degeneracy between the scattering time and
its frequency scaling that defeats any single-band fit, which we exploit with a
simultaneous two-band fit for a shared sightline pair
$(\tau_{1\,\mathrm{GHz}},\beta)$, where $\beta$ is the spectral index of the
electron-density fluctuation spectrum $P_n(q)\propto q^{-\beta}$ from which both
the pulse-broadening function shape and the frequency-scaling index
$\alpha=2\beta/(\beta-2)$ follow. We decompose each observed DM into Milky-Way
(disk and halo), cosmological (Macquart), intervening circumgalactic, and host
contributions, sourcing the intervening term from a foreground-galaxy search that
returns 49 candidate systems along nine sightlines (29 confirmed foreground);
sightlines outside a survey's footprint are treated as unconstrained by that
survey, never as foreground-free. The host residual is formally negative only
for the two highest-redshift sightlines, bounding the sightline-to-sightline
scatter about the Macquart mean. The uniform $\beta$-based re-fit is complete
across the sample: two sightlines return interior turbulence spectra
($\beta=3.72$ and $\beta=3.23$, bracketing the Kolmogorov value), nine are
consistent with the square-law limit and are quoted as geometry-conditioned
$\alpha=4$ limits under the thin-screen closure, and one fails the joint-fit
quality gate. Comparing each measured $\tau_{1\,\mathrm{GHz}}$ against the
scattering predicted for its confirmed foreground systems, the prediction
falls $1.5$--$3$ orders of magnitude short on ten of eleven sightlines,
pointing to host-dominated scattering---except FRB~20230913A, where the
foreground halo predicts $\approx$70\% of the measurement and, uniquely in
the sample, the scintillation--scattering product
$\tau\,\Delta\nu_d$ is consistent with a single screen. A
diffractive-scintillation excess of $\sim$7--11$\times$ the NE2025 Galactic floor
on the mid-latitude sightlines, robust across scattering-calibrated
electron-density models, likewise indicates an extragalactic scattering screen
rather than a Milky-Way origin. For eight sightlines with a spectroscopic host
redshift and a quality-passing joint fit we measure band-restricted
isotropic-equivalent energies of
$5\times10^{38}$--$1\times10^{41}\,\mathrm{erg}$. Finally, we show that unmodeled
temporal sub-components can bias the recovered turbulence spectrum by several
units in $\beta$, because a missing component is absorbed into the scattering tail
and distorts both the fitted pulse-broadening function shape and its frequency
scaling simultaneously.
\end{abstract}
