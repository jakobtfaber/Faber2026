\begin{abstract}
We present a joint dispersion- and scattering-budget analysis of the twelve fast
radio bursts (FRBs) co-detected by CHIME/FRB ($0.4$--$0.8$\,GHz) and DSA-110
($\sim$1.4\,GHz) between 2022 February and 2024 February, spanning
$\mathrm{DM_{obs}}=262$--$960\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$. The $\sim$1\,GHz baseline
between the two detections breaks the degeneracy between the scattering time and
its frequency scaling that defeats any single-band fit, which we exploit with a
simultaneous two-band fit for a shared sightline pair
$(\tau_{1\,\mathrm{GHz}},\beta)$, where $\beta$ is the spectral index of the
electron-density fluctuation spectrum $P_n(q)\propto q^{-\beta}$ from which both
the pulse-broadening function shape and the frequency-scaling index
$\alpha=2\beta/(\beta-2)$ follow. We decompose each observed DM into Milky-Way
(disk and halo), cosmological (Macquart), intervening circumgalactic, and host
contributions, sourcing the intervening term from a foreground-galaxy search that
returns 49 candidate systems along nine sightlines (29 confirmed foreground). An
intervening column is significant only for the two sightlines that pierce a
foreground galaxy's interior; the host residual is formally negative for three
sightlines (including that pair), thereby bounding the circumgalactic model. For
eight sightlines with a spectroscopic host redshift and a quality-passing joint
fit we measure band-restricted isotropic-equivalent energies of
$5\times10^{38}$--$1\times10^{41}\,\mathrm{erg}$. A
diffractive-scintillation excess of $\sim$7--11$\times$ the NE2025 Galactic floor
on the mid-latitude sightlines, robust across scattering-calibrated
electron-density models, indicates an extragalactic (host or intervening)
scattering screen rather than a Milky-Way origin. Finally, we show that unmodeled
temporal sub-components can bias the recovered turbulence spectrum by several
units in $\beta$, because a missing component is absorbed into the scattering tail
and distorts both the fitted pulse-broadening function shape and its frequency
scaling simultaneously. Fixed-s² component adjudication is complete for the four
Tier-B sightlines; population-level $\beta$ statistics and the
measured-versus-predicted budget overlay remain qualitative pending FINAL figure
regeneration in the \texttt{dsa110-FLITS} pipeline.
\end{abstract}
