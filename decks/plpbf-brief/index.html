Power-law PBF model change — review brief

Owner-approved 2026-07-17 · reference: Cordes, Ocker, Chatterjee et al. 2025 (PTA noise processes preprint) · figures below are theirs, annotations are ours

The change in three sentences

Our joint fit convolves a Gaussian burst with an exponential pulse-broadening function (PBF), which thin-screen theory permits only in the β→4 limit where the scaling index is exactly α=4 — so the current family can express β<4 only with an unbounded t−β/2 tail, which the data reject, and the fit flees to the rail. The approved change adds one physical parameter, the inner-scale ratio ζ = li/ld, which truncates the tail at τi = ζ−2τ — decoupling tail slope (β) from tail extent (ζ), so a steep-α, β<4 screen can finally coexist with a finite tail. The family nests our current model exactly (ζ ≳ 10 ⇒ pure exponential), so the evidence comparison is clean.

Fig 40 — what the PBF actually looks like (the shape we cannot currently express)

Thin screen, Kolmogorov β=11/3. Three regimes: exponential core, t−β/2 power-law tail, inner-scale exponential cutoff. The tail extent is set by ζ: at ζ=0.01 it spans four decades; at ζ=10 (red) the curve is indistinguishable from the pure exponential (black) — our current EMG is this one limiting curve of the whole family. These four curves are the acceptance test for the implementation on h17.

Fig 54 — the bias mechanism at one frequency

Fit an exponential-PBF template to a pulse actually scattered by the power-law PBF (ζ=0.01): the recovered τ overestimates truth by up to ~5×, worst when τ is small relative to the pulse width. This is the injection-harness positive control: our EMG fits on PL-PBF injections must reproduce these curves before we trust any recovery result.

Fig 58 — why a railed or sub-4 α can be manufactured by the wrong PBF

Same mismatch, now across frequency. The apparent scaling index xτ starts at the true 4.4 and collapses — below 4, down to ~2.2 — as frequency and pulse-width ratio grow. A fitted α<4 from an exponential-PBF model is a shape-mismatch signature, not a measurement of exotic turbulence; no homogeneous thin screen produces α<4 under any PBF. This is how we will read the relaxed-α A/B: α stays ≥4 → the ν−4 rail is data-driven; α drops → the exponential shape is wrong, and the PL-PBF lane is the fix.

Fig 55 — what it means for arrival times (the TOA stakes)

Systematic TOA shift from template mismatch, vs τ/W and ζ. Heavy-tailed PBFs shift arrival-time estimates by up to ~2.5×τ relative to exponential-PBF assumptions. Since our TOA table inherits whatever PBF the joint fit assumes, this quantifies the systematic we are currently absorbing into inflated error bars on the shape-mismatch bursts (casey, wilhelm).

Predictions the family must survive

(1) Chromatic tail: ζ(ν) ∝ ν−2/(β−2) — the tail is shorter at CHIME frequencies, longer in the DSA band, which is testable against exactly where the casey/wilhelm residual structures live. (2) Nesting: with ζ free, bursts that are genuinely exponential should recover ζ ≳ 10 with no evidence penalty. (3) Injection recovery: known (τ, β, ζ) recovered unbiased, and EMG-on-PL injections reproduce Figs 54/58. Gate order: injection harness → casey+wilhelm evidence comparison → owner review → sample-wide.
