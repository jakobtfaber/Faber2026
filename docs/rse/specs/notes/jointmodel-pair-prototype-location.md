## Fit-quality flags noticed during visual review

The prototype figures are diagnostic. As of the 2026-07-07 visual review, do
not treat the following current fit artifacts as adequate burst-shape models:

- `hamilton`: CHIME data show two components; the current shared-zeta fit is
  effectively C1D1 and misses the leading CHIME component.
- `whitney`: DSA data show two narrow components; the current C2D2 beta-native
  fit is degenerate for DSA (`zeta_D1` is very broad) and misses the second
  visible DSA component.
- `wilhelm`: the current shared-zeta fit misses leading DSA structure and leaves
  a coherent bright-pulse residual. This is residual pulse-profile structure
  within the beta≈4 exponential/EMG-preferred branch, not evidence against the
  EMG/exponential branch itself.

These rows need multiplicity or residual-structure re-fitting before being used
as accepted model figures. The renderer emits warnings for these bursts when
regenerated.

Resolved since the initial visual review:

- `johndoeII`: the old C2D1 beta-campaign product is superseded by the promoted
  C2D2 product (`beta=3.936`, `alpha=4.07` as a railed-hi limit,
  `tau_1GHz=2.219 ms`, `chi2_C/D=1.09/1.23`). The renderer no longer warns for
  this burst.
- `zach`: the old C1D1 beta-campaign product is superseded by the promoted
  C2D4 diagnostic product (`beta=3.990`, `alpha=4.00` as a beta-limit product,
  `tau_1GHz=0.186 ms`, `chi2_C/D=1.35/1.02`). The new figure models the DSA
  trailing cluster as one initial pulse plus three trailing components and uses
  Zach-specific time padding to keep the trailing structure in frame. A DSA
  initial-pulse residual remains a caveat.
