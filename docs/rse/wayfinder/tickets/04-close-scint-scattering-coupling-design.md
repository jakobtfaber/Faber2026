## Question

The modular constraint-layer design (legacy A1) is a working draft the owner
re-opened the night it was adopted: scintillation products enter as frozen
posterior/limit products with quality flags; screen counting is
probabilistic; a second broadening component is fitted only if an escalation
trigger fires. Since then the evidence-based trigger clause (nested-sampling
ACF model comparison) was retired for want of a usable operating point,
leaving posterior-predictive profile residuals as the sole escalation limb.
With the ratified CHIME-band method fixing what the scintillation products
actually are, does the owner now close the design: (a) posterior-predictive
residuals as the only trigger — sufficient, or does it need a calibrated
false-escalation rate of its own? (b) the product interface contract (formats,
quality flags, censoring conventions) as drafted? (c) prior-odds-only role
for scintillation geometry in kernel-family selection? Closing this unblocks
the geometry-model-selection design work that the re-fit campaign consumes.
